DIALOGUE FLOOR: 2% dialogue, the lowest in the book, in the chapter that carries the first onset of the quiet and the motor-control solution. This is the emotional turning point of the first act and it is the most solitary chapter you have written.

Also note the chapter contains one of the two best lines in the manuscript: "For twenty-two minutes, he was the person he had believed competence would eventually allow him to become."

It lands because the clinical register drops for exactly one sentence, and it is the only place in the chapter that happens. Worth making that a deliberate technique rather than an accident: roughly one register break per chapter, placed at the peak, and nowhere else. Used everywhere it would stop working, which is why the surrounding restraint has to stay.
