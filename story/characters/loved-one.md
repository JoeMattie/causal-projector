## Established continuity (Chapter 1)

- Ruth is Evan's mother and a former community-college mathematics teacher.
  She retains a precise, dry sense of humor and dislikes being watched while
  taking medication.
- Her husband died four years before the opening. She planned his funeral and
  knew the facts of his death, but sometimes asks when he will be home for
  dinner.
- Her cognition is variable rather than uniformly absent. She catches an
  error in her medication schedule, remembers a library outing Evan forgot,
  and also looks for people and places from inaccessible periods of her life.
- She has an active friendship with Marisol, who takes her to the library
  sale. Remaining at home and continuing these ordinary choices matter to her
  independently of Evan's need to keep her safe.
- Evan's garage apparatus is, in Ruth's preferred shorthand, the machine that
  takes pictures of thoughts. Their exchanges are affectionate, impatient,
  and intellectually familiar rather than clinical.

## Established continuity (Chapters 6-7)

- Ruth recognizes Evan's first deliberately selected but useless outcome as
  practice, seeing the behavioral threshold before he names it that way.
- The home-care agency approves twelve weekly hours on Tuesday and Thursday
  mornings plus alternating Saturdays. Ruth chooses the schedule herself and
  corrects Evan when he optimizes for supervision rather than her preferences.
- She removes Evan's new cupboard labels, resisting his effort to make her home
  conform to a single orderly system.

## Established continuity (Chapter 9)

- After an episode sends her to a defunct bus route for a class she no longer
  teaches, Ruth requests one six-minute reduced exposure. She records informed
  instructions, defines her own stop conditions, and cuts Lena's numbered seal
  herself, while recognizing that Evan wants the result.
- The run gives Ruth roughly forty-three minutes of sustained orientation,
  reasoning, and autobiographical coherence. She uses that clarity to state her
  own care preferences rather than simply performing wellness for Evan.
- She also remembers an older daughter, Celia, whose detailed life is integrated
  across family history but absent from Evan's memory and current records. Ruth
  experiences Celia's absence as a second real loss, not a puzzle for Evan to
  correct.

## Established continuity (Chapter 11)

- Ruth requires the working model to describe Celia as absent from Evan's
  records rather than as a wrong version, refusing to let his history serve as
  the sole authenticator of hers.

## Dementia as loss of overlap

In the fictional model, dementia is not simply “having too few copies.”
Different cognitive subsystems remain supported by different history bundles,
but their shared intersection shrinks. Language, autobiographical memory,
face recognition, emotional association, and temporal orientation no longer
align within enough complete histories to decode one stable self.

B_self  =  B_memory intersect B_language intersect B_perception intersect B_emotion

As this intersection becomes sparse, damaged inputs are reconstructed as the
nearest surviving codeword. Years alias together. One loved person decodes as
another. A plausible memory from an adjacent family history fills a gap.
Lucid intervals occur when the bundles temporarily realign.

## Character and ethical consequence

A treatment run can produce genuine lucidity while restoring the wrong
coherent version. The relative knows the protagonist but remembers a sibling
he never had or a childhood accident from another history. The machine has
not healed the original person; it has selected a version that still closes
consistently. The relative must retain dignity, desire, and agency rather
than serving as a cosmic clue dispenser.

## Disaster 2: The cure is a substitution

The dementia-affected relative becomes lucid but remembers another life. A
coherent implementation has been selected, not restored.

## Guardrail

Dementia is not a sign of lesser worth, weak will, or metaphysical scarcity.
The relative remains a person with independent aims and moral standing.
