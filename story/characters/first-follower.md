Consolidated from the development dossier and continuity established in Chapter
7, "The Short List." The dossier remains the source for future planning.

## Working design

A friend, partner, colleague, patient, or online collaborator experiences a
genuine benefit from the machine: relief from anxiety, cognitive clarity, or
a brief return of memory. They become the sincere advocate who scales the
effect. This person is not a fool or cultist. Their benefit is real, which
makes their support dangerous and prevents the conflict from collapsing into
“sensible people versus mad scientist.” The role can merge with the
collaborator or remain distinct depending on cast size.

## Established continuity (Chapter 7)

- Nadia manages six controls engineers and reviews Evan's work at the motor
  bench.
- Evan's estimator rewrite prevents a dangerous zero-speed failure and holds up
  under tests Nadia and her team can run themselves.
- Nadia moves the release date forward, gives Evan technical ownership of the
  recovery, and reorganizes the team around his five-gate plan.
- Her support follows useful work and ordinary evidence. She hasn't been
  exposed to the projector and doesn't know it exists.

## Planned pressure

Interpersonal: the collaborator demands restraint, the relative resists being
optimized, and followers sincerely want continued access.

Nadia's benefit is real, which is exactly why her later support can become
dangerous. She shouldn't turn into a cultist or a convenient villain. The open
question is when useful engineering confidence becomes a path for the
projector's control pattern to spread.
