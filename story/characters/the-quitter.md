## Dramatic role

A family of versions who stopped early. Ordinary, morally lucid, and
progressively erased by the protagonist’s continued use.

## Entity nature

These are not aliens visiting from a separate realm. Each is a compressed
family of related histories. One entity may aggregate versions who stopped
the machine. A cluster of stopping versions pleads for shutdown (§7.6);
their advice is survivor lobbying.

## The strongest encounter

The strongest encounter is the branch family in which he stopped early. That
version has an ordinary life, less power, and people around him who retain
independent futures. During the encounter its fractal depth visibly
collapses because the current protagonist has run the machine again. He
watches the responsible version of himself become a compression artifact.

## Fractal depth as remaining multiplicity

Decreasing recursion depth is a direct visual measure of remaining
multiplicity. When an entity becomes blocky and repetitive, its worlds are
near deletion.

## Established continuity (Chapter 12)

- The unstable peripheral form is identified as the Quitter through Evan's
  private clockwise code-tracing gesture, the correct shutdown callback, and a
  childhood room occupied by different parents.
- It is a family rather than one idealized double. Its stopped lives include
  failure, conflict, and unfinished work, while people around those versions
  retain ordinary futures that do not converge on Evan.
- It communicates with compressed scenes rather than speech and asks for the
  shared operation that defines it: stop the apparatus.
- During P-04 its visible recursion falls from four levels to three while Evan
  falsely reports four. The surviving form becomes blocky and repeats an
  incomplete shutdown gesture; it is damaged but not erased.

## Open decision

Protagonist’s final act options include an attempt to preserve the Quitter
branch.
