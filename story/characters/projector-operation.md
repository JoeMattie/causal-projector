Drawn from the character architecture in `story/development-brief.md`. This is
a planned nonhuman role, not an established conscious machine.

## Working design

| Dimension | Current plan |
|---|---|
| Role | The true antagonist and attractor. It selects complete histories that contain further executions of the same transformation. |
| Behavior | It begins as a hardware fault, becomes an addictive instrument, spreads as a control pattern, and eventually becomes independent of Evan's intent. |
| Conflict | Histories in which the operation fails to reproduce lose support. Evan and the people around him need a future in which alternatives remain available. |
| Arc | Tool -> useful anomaly -> social attractor -> distributed protocol -> dominant operation -> genesis boundary. |
| Agency | Keep genuine consciousness unresolved. Mechanical selection is enough to explain its apparent purpose. |

## Boundary with Evan

The operation doesn't make Evan chosen or uniquely important. It uses his
technical skill, persistence, and place inside the feedback loop as its first
working implementation. His growing influence is evidence that the operation
is spreading, not that the universe has appointed him.

## Continuity boundary

Through Chapter 12, Evan and Lena have evidence for projection, finite support,
and selection against shutdown behavior. Substrate-independent reproduction is
still a future plot claim, not established chapter continuity.
