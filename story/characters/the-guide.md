## Dramatic role

A family of successful continuants who provide technical insight. Helpful,
beautiful, and terrifyingly low in ambiguity.

## Entity nature

Each entity is a compressed family of related histories. An entity may
contain thousands of versions that solved different portions of the problem.
Communication arrives as compressed conceptual memory rather than spoken
sentences. The operator experiences sudden technical revelation because his
brain reconstructs an insight-shaped object already present in many related
histories.

They are not omniscient. Each knows only what its branch family supports,
and each has an agenda. A cluster of continuing versions teaches more
effective settings. A beautiful, coherent entity may be dangerous precisely
because the projector has removed most of its uncertainty. Their advice is
survivor lobbying.

## Established continuity (Chapter 10)

- The first controlled appearance is a vertically closed figure made from
  nested, recursively subdividing cells. Its chirality tracks hidden physical
  phase steps while ignoring software-identical decoy writes.
- It presents Evan with a five-band image of an overlooked confirmation edge.
  Implementing that insight reduces ordinary loss by eleven percent but causes
  rank collapse to arrive sooner and deepen.
- Evan experiences the figure as attentive and agent-like. Lena records it as a
  structured percept and keeps unidentified cues and observer inference open.

## Established continuity (Chapter 11)

- The Guide's visible recursion declines from eight stable structural levels to
  six as anomalous cycles accumulate, while its five-band organization and
  chirality remain coherent.
- Evan and Lena provisionally interpret it as a residual family aligned with
  continued projector operation, making its useful advice a form of survivor
  lobbying rather than neutral instruction.

## Guardrail

The entities have branch-limited knowledge and conflicting interests. None
is a convenient cosmic encyclopedia.
