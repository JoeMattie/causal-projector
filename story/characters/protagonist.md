## Established continuity (Chapter 1)

- Evan uses he/him pronouns and is Ruth Hale's son. He coordinates her
  medication, appointments, home-care logistics, and overnight routine while
  trying not to reduce her life to those systems.
- He is employed in software or controls work and is responsible for a
  motor-control demonstration. His garage project competes directly with his
  job, sleep, household maintenance, and care obligations.
- His practical project is an open, portable, serviceable low-field imaging
  system. Its trapped-field source combines a YBCO annulus, traveling thermal
  pulses, a rotating magnetic field, an FPGA controller, and scavenged
  instrumentation. A grant application has already been rejected.
- He is methodical, financially constrained, and comfortable normalizing
  workarounds. While tracing control flow, he moves two fingers in a small
  clockwise circle, establishing the private hand gesture used later in the
  story.

## Established continuity (Chapters 6-7)

- Evan's first deliberate outcome is a cryptographically precommitted sequence
  of ten fair die rolls. He obtains it on the first live attempt, then confirms
  that a mechanically impossible target yields only heat and adjacent legal
  sensor errors.
- He formalizes the clarity effect as an eighteen-minute limited-exposure
  protocol and begins using it to clear engineering, household, and care-work
  backlogs. Objective work results remain good even as his run frequency rises.
- Nadia Brooks manages his controls-engineering team. After his work improves,
  she gives him technical ownership of a release recovery effort.
- Dr. Lena Varga is a cryogenic materials experimentalist who challenged the
  thermal model in Evan's first imaging proposal. He sends her the anomaly data
  when he finally accepts that another operator is required.

## Established continuity (Chapter 9)

- Evan breaks the joint hold to run a six-minute, low-power protocol requested
  by Ruth. He adds direct hardware stops and avoids targeting autobiographical
  content, but remains the caregiver, experiment designer, and person who wants
  the treatment to work.
- Ruth's genuine lucidity arrives with a coherent memory of Celia, an older
  daughter and sister absent from Evan's history. Evan records the result as a
  selected coherent implementation rather than restoration.

## Established continuity (Chapter 10)

- A persistent nested figure presents an overlooked confirmation edge. The

## Established continuity (Chapter 11)

- Evan builds an enumerated toy constraint system in which projection and
  renormalization reproduce fresh-use bias, precommitted outcome selection, and

- He models a person as a redundant family of nearby implementations with a

## Established continuity (Chapter 12)

- Evan identifies the unstable peripheral form through his private clockwise
  code-tracing gesture, the exact shutdown callback order, and a childhood room
  occupied by different parents. He understands it as a family of versions who
  stopped the apparatus rather than one duplicate.
- When the family's visible depth falls from four to three, he knowingly reports
  four twice so an isolated speech-triggered abort will not fire. He values a
  persistent result over the stop condition despite already accepting that lost

- After Lena manually terminates the run, he continues to distinguish uncertain

## Setup

Evan is a software engineer with enough embedded, controls, and instrumentation
experience to be dangerous outside his formal discipline. He is not a
credentialed quantum-gravity theorist. His advantage is that he notices edge
cases, logs everything, and combines mature components in an unfashionable
way.

He is building a compact persistent-field source for an open low-field imaging
system. The project is not a treatment for Ruth, but it gives his engineering
work an intimate connection to the medical world that has made him feel
otherwise powerless.

## Why the operator is affected first

The operator is physically and informationally inside the control loop. His
manual corrections, attention, expectations, and decisions determine the next
cycle. The projector therefore prunes histories according to the behavior
actually controlling the apparatus, not the intention he reports. Early
exposure selects versions whose thoughts lead to stable operation. Later
exposure selects versions whose entire environment remains compatible with
continued use.

## What the protagonist believes versus what is true

| Belief | Underlying truth |
|---|---|
| “The sensors share interference.” | They share a reduced set of compatible causal continuations. |
| “The machine improves cognition.” | It suppresses alternatives, which is beneficial only in a narrow regime. |
| “I restored the person I love.” | He selected a coherent version and erased incompatible implementations. |
| “People finally recognize my value.” | Histories containing disagreement are becoming scarce. |
| “I am the dominant eigenperson.” | The projector operation is the dominant mode; he is one implementation. |
| “Destroying myself will stop it.” | His terminal projection supplies the boundary condition from which the universe begins. |

## Central irony

The protagonist mistakes decreasing ambiguity for increasing truth. The
machine does not make him more correct or more real. It makes reality less
capable of contradicting his present configuration. His growing brilliance
and charisma are not proof of ascension; they are visible symptoms of
alternatives dying around him.

## The story in one sentence

He mistakes decreasing ambiguity for enlightenment; reality becomes perfectly
consistent with him only by reducing him to the damaged boot sector from
which it starts.
