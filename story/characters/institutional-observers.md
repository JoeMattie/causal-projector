Consolidated from the development dossier. No institution has detected the
anomaly through Chapter 12, so this remains future planning.

## Working design

A university lab, employer, medical group, intelligence unit, or open-source
community may detect the anomaly. Avoid generic villains arriving solely to
weaponize it. The more distinctive possibility is that institutions
independently reconstruct the same topology because the projector is
selecting histories in which it reproduces. Bureaucratic adoption becomes a
symptom rather than an external conspiracy.

| Dimension | Current plan |
|---|---|
| Goal | Reliable prediction, safer systems, treatment, competitive advantage, or control of a result that cannot remain private. |
| Fear | A useful technology that cannot be governed, reproduced safely, or contained inside one organization. |
| Arc | Skepticism -> interest -> replication -> convergence on the same topology -> carrier of the operation after the garage hardware is gone. |
| Constraint | Institutional actions must remain locally reasonable. Bureaucracy is a transmission path, not a cartoon villain. |

## Institutions as eigenpatterns

Often the strongest modes are institutions, protocols, markets, religions,
bureaucracies, standards, or self-reproducing machines. The protagonist’s
fatal mistake is to assume the world’s growing alignment means he has become
the dominant eigenvector.

## Guardrail

Transformative people are not inherently good, chosen, or more real.
Institutions and systems may dominate them.

## Open decision

Public exposure: secret garage tragedy, open-source replication cascade,
institutional scale-up, or a hybrid in which the pattern spreads without
public understanding.
